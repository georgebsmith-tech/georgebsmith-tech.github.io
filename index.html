Example 3.1

Given the Sequence \(7, 5, 3,...\), determine the
i. Sum of the 10th and 20th terms.
ii. Sum of the first nine terms of the sequence.

Solution 3.1

Taking a close look at the sequence, you will notice that it's an Arithmetic progression. Reason being that there is a common difference between consecutive terms as illustrated below.

The first term \(T_1=7\), the second term is \(T_2=5\) and the third term is \(T_3=3\), hence the common difference is \[d=T_2-T_1=T_3-T_2\]. Now \[T_2-T_1=5-7=-2\] and \[T_3-T_2=3-5=-2\]. The difference are same, hence the common difference is \[d=-2\]. Now that we have the first term \(a=7\) and the common difference \(d=-2\), we can proceed to answered the questions given to us.
i. The formula for the nth term of an AP is \(\;\;T_n=a+(n-1)d,\) \[\Rightarrow T_{10}=7+(10-1)(-2)=7-18\] \[T_{10}=-11\] \( and\;T_{20}=7+(20-1)(-2)=7-38\)
\[T_{20}=-31\]. The sum of the 10t and 20th terms is \[sum=-11+(-31)=-42\] ii. For the sum of the first nine terms, we have to make use of the formula, \[S_n=\frac{n}{2}(2a+(n-1)d),\] hence \(S_9=\frac{9}{2}(2(7)+(9-1)(-2))\)
\(S_9=\frac{9}{2}(14-16)=-9\)
